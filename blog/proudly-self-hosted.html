Proudly self-hosted

Proudly self-hosted   self hosting

<div class="pubdate"> November 20, 2016 </div>

Over the last years, I've been increasingly uncomfortable with the idea of hosting my personal data on someone else's computer: email, calendar & contacts hosting at Google, file hosting at Dropbox, snippets at Pastebin, source code at GitHub, etc.

When talking about it with colleagues and friends, I would often get the answer that it's just so convenient: nothing to maintain, it's "free" (really, it's not), always available, services sync your data on your computer & phone, etc.

Sure, all of that is true, but at the cost of my privacy, and that's a price I'm simply not ready to pay anymore.

I've been off Google & Dropbox for quite some time now already, but I've decided to go one (big) step further with self-hosting.

Yunohost

The main issue when it comes to hosting your data & services yourself is the time it takes: you have to maintain your server, monitor it, keep it up-to-date, think about security, back up your data, make sure there's as little downtime as possible, and so on. All of that takes a lot of time and energy.

I recently discovered an awesome project that tries really hard to make self-hosting easier: Yunohost.

The concept is simple: provide a GNU/Linux system that makes self-hosting less of a pain. The distribution is based on Debian and provides pre-configured and packaged services that are easier to install, configure and maintain.

Yunohost offers a nice Web UI to administrate the box, as well as a CLI tool. Both make it easy to install & configure applications and services, as well as user management and access rights.

The right hardware

Now that I settled on the software to use, I had to pick the hardware as well. There are many options out there, from the regular PC to the tiny ARM board.

What was really important was to have something fan-less, with low power consumption, but still powerful enough to run all of my services (a dozen) without any issue.

It turns out there is a little brother project of Yunohost, the Internet Cube: a small yet powerful ARM board on which you install Yunohost.

Privacy & internet connection

Huge bonus point, the Internet Cube comes with a VPN client & Wifi hotspot application, so you can use an ethical and neutral internet connection at home, provided that you register to an ethical internet provider with VPN access.

Packaging these two applications in the Cube makes it a two-fold device offering both a clean Internet connection respecting your privacy over Wifi and self-hosting, perfect!
